KEROSENE & NOX TANK WEIGHING TEST STAND

DYNAMIC DATA LOGGING FOR DUKE AERO'S LIQUID PROPULSION SYSTEM

Key Features

The frame is designed using the Frame feature on Onshape, constructed from 80/20 extruded Aluminum, providing a robust and modular structure. It is designed to hold the 3 tanks (Nitrogen, Nitrous Oxide (NOX), Kerosene) stable during testing. The front bars of the frame are removable to allow for easy mounting and removal of the tanks.

The load cells are used to monitor the weight of the kerosene and NOX tanks during testing. The load cells are 100lb S-type, mounted between the bottom of the frame and a supporting plate with 1/2"-13 bolts.

The supporting plate has 3 holes for 3 threaded rods serving as standoff to make room for valves and piping at the bottom of the tanks. The standoffs support a 3D printed holder which the tank sits on. The hole in the holder piece is for piping from the bottom tank closure.

The final feature is these supporting braces. They are 3D printed and can be redesigned to adapt to different tank sizes. The braces are mounted onto the frame with T-slot nuts and can be slid in or out to access the tank.
